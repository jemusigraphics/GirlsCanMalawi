About Us

Girls Can Malawi is an initiative that aims to empower girls through access to quality education. We believe that through education, girls can contribute significantly to the nation's development.

Girls can end the cycle of poverty, have better health outcomes, and drive gender equality. For us, investing in girls' education is not just a matter of social justice—it is a strategic investment in the future of Malawi.

Why Focus on Girls?

Girls have historically experienced prejudice and had fewer chances in a variety of fields, including healthcare, work, and education. One of the most vulnerable groups in society is girls.

Making girls a priority is a calculated attempt to address and correct these differences, advancing gender parity and empowering girls. contest preconceptions and traditional gender roles.

HOW WE DO IT

MENSTRUAL HYGIENE

Due to limited access to sanitary products, girls in primary and secondary school fail to concentrate in class. The lack of proper sanitation facilities in schools hinders girls' education as they miss classes during their periods. Cultural beliefs and taboos surrounding menstruation further compound the issue of menstrual hygiene.

We provide menstrual health education, teach girls how to make reusable menstrual pads, and ensuring the availability of clean and private sanitation facilities. We also aim to conduct community-based awareness to combat myths and promoting understanding, encouraging girls to adopt healthier menstrual hygiene practices without fear or shame.

SENDING GIRLS BACK TO SCHOOL

We encourage girls who have dropped out of school to go back to school. We do not provide bursaries to those who facing financial constraints, however we provide school necessities like school uniform, notebooks and pens.

Sending girls back to school is a catalyst for social and cultural transformation. Education plays a crucial role in promoting the health and well-being of girls and their communities. Investing in the education of girls enhance economic development. Sending girls back to school challenges traditional gender roles and stereotypes that perpetuate inequality.

MENTORING

We provide a structured and supportive environment for girls to navigate the challenges they face. Our mentorship programs focus on areas such as education, sexual and reproductive health, entrepreneurship, and leadership development.

We serve as role models, demonstrating that girls can aspire to and achieve any goal they set for themselves. Our mentorship programs contribute to the academic success of girls through providing guidance on educational pathways, career choices, and skill development.
